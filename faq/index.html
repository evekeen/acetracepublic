F.A.Q.

How do I add a line?

Free Curve - manual

In manual mode you can mark a few points, and the app will create a smooth line using then.

  • Scroll the video to find the object
  • Select the object by moving the cursor. You can zoom-in with 2 fingers
  • Scroll the video again and mark the object by moving the cursor
  • Add 4 to 10 key points through the flight
  • Tap Preview to preview the result

Disc Golf - automatic

  • Scroll to the beginning of the flight
  • Select a disc by the rectangular frame
  • Tap the Start Tracking button

Golf

  • Scroll the video to find the start of the flight
  • Select the ball by moving the cursor
  • Adjust the landing point by moving the yellow ring
  • Adjust the shape of the trajectory by moving control points
  • Change the flight duration by resizing the bar in the timeline
  • Adjust the acceleration - make the opaque line end match the current frame ball position

Can Ace Trace do it automatically?

The automatic tracking for disc golf is fully supported on iOS. Check it out! We’re working on supporting other sports (golf, etc) and bringing that to the Android platform.

Do I need to buy it?

You can play with the app and see the results completely for free. In order to save resulting video to your gallery you need to buy a subscription or buy individual video exports (iOS). Also, on iOS we offer one free video every 35 days.

Why not free?

  • We cannot afford to make it free. Free products made by big companies usually sell user’s data for advertising.
  • When a product is free, you are the product. We have no intention of selling client’s data since we consider that being evil
  • We won’t add ads into the app, since we believe users must enjoy a good product without being bombarded with annoying banners.

Thank you for your understanding, your support is very important for us!

Do you have free options?

We do offer free subscriptions for school students or people with low income in certain cases. Feel free to send us a message describing your situation. Also, on iOS there is a free video available every 35 days.

Are there any limitation?

Android

  • Only certain (standard) video dimensions work due to Android video codec limitations
  • Sometimes first 10-20 frames of a video might not be scrollable in the app if the video was trimmed in some other software. Try playing around with trimming and see if it works
  • In some videos a line is out of synch with the video (in the exported Ace Trace video it starts before the throw). Try resampling the original video to a different format or just use other videos.
  • Video codecs fail for variety of reasons on different platforms, and we cannot do anything about it - it’s the device manufacturers and Android platform who should fix these errors. Sometimes the best solution is to just upgrade your device. Also, usually videos that were filmed on the device work better.
  • Video stabilization is slow on Android due to inability to read a video file in several parallel threads unlike on iOS.
  • There is no sound in the exported video on Android (we’re working on that)
  • Distance editor is not available on Android (we’re working on that)
  • Automatic tracking is not available on Android (we’re working on that)
